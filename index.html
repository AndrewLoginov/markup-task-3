Lucky horseshoe

legend of the Wild West

One day a black horseman came to a Texas ranch with a black stallion. He appealed to the local blacksmith Dukkan with a request to shoe.

But his request was very unusual: he asked to shoe not his horse, but himself

The old blacksmith immediately realized that we were dealing with the "unclean" himself. He took the longest nails and began to nail horseshoes. Every nail has caused the devil such suffering that he pleaded for mercy. Then Dukkan took a bullet from him to never enter the house where a horseshoe hangs above the door.

Ever since, horseshoe is considered a symbol of good luck and a talisman against evil forces.

In the bag

Indians had little reason to love white people. The "pale-faced" drove the Redskins from their lands, providing in exchange fruitless reservations. White hunters ruthlessly exterminated the buffalo, the hunt for which was for many tribes the main source of food. For the warlike Indians of the West, war seemed the easiest and most natural way to end the “white threat”. The Indians did not realize how unequal the forces were. Each tribe was in contact with a very limited number of “pale faces”, and few Indians visited cities on the Atlantic coast. The number of American soldiers who participated in the wars with the Indians has also always been very small. As a result, the Indians were inclined to downplay the total number of white people, considering them to be a relatively small, albeit very powerful tribe. Therefore, even small Indian tribes often declared war on the United States, counting on a favorable outcome for themselves. The American government seized upon every opportunity to rid the western lands of their native inhabitants, which caused so much inconvenience to the white settlers. Most whites were in complete agreement with General Sheridan’s statement in the epigraph of this chapter.



Thus, wars between whites and Indians were inevitable. In 1862, the troops of Colonel Christopher "Whale" Carson attacked the Apaches in their reservations. This war continued intermittently until 1886, when the Apaches finally ceased resistance, and their leader Geronimo surrendered to captivity. Keith Carson, acting together with the Utah Indians, campaigned against the Navajo. He burned the Navajo settlements and stole their livestock. Somewhat later, he failed to defeat the combined forces of the Kiowa, Cheyenne and Comanche in the battle of Edowie Walls. Colonel Carson was killed by the Indians in 1868. The most successful war for the Indians was the Red Cloud War, named after the Lakota leader. According to the peace treaty in Fort Larami in 1868, the Lakota received a huge reservation, in the territory of which there should not have been American troops, settlements and roads. American soldiers, having at their disposal only 53 warriors. After gold was discovered on the Lakota reservation territory, the contract at Fort Laramie turned into an empty piece of paper. Numerous violations of this agreement by whites forced the Indians in 1876 to re-enter the warpath. In the first major battle of Little Bighorn, the combined forces of the Lakota and Cheyennes, commanded by the leaders of Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse, defeated the 7th Cavalry Regiment of the US Army. The hero of the Civil War, commanded by American troops, General George Custer was surrounded with a small detachment of his soldiers during the battle and was killed in battle. The American cavalrymen lost more than 300 people killed and wounded, the Indians a little less.
